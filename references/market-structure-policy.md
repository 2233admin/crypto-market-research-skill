# Market Structure Policy

Crypto research must consider market structure before making directional or factor-like claims.

## Required Checks

- Spot trend and volume context
- Perpetual funding and open interest
- Futures basis where relevant
- Liquidation or leverage concentration where available
- Order-book or liquidity-zone context where available
- Options implied volatility and skew where relevant
- Stablecoin supply, flows, or exchange reserves where relevant
- On-chain activity, protocol usage, and token supply mechanics where relevant
- Macro-liquidity and regulatory catalysts when material

## Trade Planning Discipline

When the user asks for entries, stops, or trade plans:

1. Separate higher-timeframe bias from lower-timeframe execution.
2. Treat liquidity levels as sweep/decision zones, not automatic entries.
3. State invalidation before targets.
4. Distinguish evidence-backed levels from speculative scenarios.
5. Mark whether data is live, delayed, or historical.

## Factor Work Discipline

For factor candidates, specify whether the factor is based on price action, derivatives positioning, on-chain data, protocol fundamentals, liquidity, volatility, or narrative/event data.
