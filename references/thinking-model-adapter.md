# Thinking Model Adapter

Use thinking models as structured reasoning aids, not accuracy guarantees.

## Default Subset

- `thinking-bayesian`: update confidence as new evidence arrives.
- `thinking-scientific-method`: convert claims into falsifiable hypotheses.
- `thinking-systems`: examine market structure, feedback loops, and cross-asset or sector linkages.
- `thinking-inversion`: identify how a thesis or factor can fail.
- `thinking-pre-mortem`: stress-test a report, thesis, or factor before publication.
- `thinking-second-order`: inspect indirect effects such as crowding, reflexivity, liquidity response, or policy spillover.
- `thinking-ooda`: update fast-moving reads when new market evidence changes the situation.

## Progressive Disclosure

Start with one or two models. Load or invoke broader thinking-model material only when the request needs it. Output the model labels, checks performed, confidence changes, and risk flags; avoid long generic essays unless the user explicitly asks for philosophical analysis.
